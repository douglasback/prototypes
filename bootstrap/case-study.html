The Challenge

Say you're the Super Bowl champion Green Bay Packers (just for instance). You've got amazing fans—but you want to find a way to generate more community spirit and strengthen everybody's relationship to the team. More involved fans means better game attendance, more merch sales, and louder cheering at games—all good things.

The Approach

Something we learned from running political campaigns: if you want to make people feel like part of something bigger, make it easy for them to get together in person. In the case of Packers fans, their local establishment every Sunday.

Blue State partnered with the Packers to recruit more than 1,800 bars across all 50 states to be official "Packers Everywhere" bars. Fans could easily search for their local Sunday game-watching spot through the Packers Everywhere website, custom mobile app, or their favorite social network.

A segmented email and content campaign run on the BSD Tools encouraged fans to contribute photos and stories about their game-day experiences and their lives as fans.
